# Transformers on Transformers

**An experiment in AI-driven creation of university-level educational materials.**

---

## Technical Overview

This project explores the possibilities of utilizing contemporary AI (specifically, **Claude Opus 4.8**) to create high-quality, university-level educational text explaining a sufficiently challenging and complex topic.

For this experiment, I chose the explanation of **transformer technology** itself and the neural network models built upon it as the core theme.

### Target Audience & Goal

The goal was to produce a text that is both **technically accurate and didactically sound**, introducing this revolutionary technology in a welcoming and comprehensible manner. It is tailored particularly for curious university students who already possess a basic understanding of:

- Artificial Neural Networks
- Linear Algebra

---

## Methodology & Didactic Approach

The text is primarily based on an in-depth, comprehensive conversation with the model. This conversation was subsequently fed back to the AI with a specialized prompt requesting the creation of a coherent educational text.

The extensive prompt emphasized a strong application of **didactic principles** and typical technical principles as well i.e.:

- **Problem-First Approach:** Explaining *what* sub-problem needs to be solved and *why* before describing the mathematical implementation.
- **Visuals & Examples:** Frequent use of illustrative visual diagrams and general explanatory examples.
- **Technical Rigor:** Every mathematical identifier must be described, providing neuro-parallels (e.g., passes through linear layers of neurons) for matrix operations wherever possible.
- **Academic Standards:** Structured numbering of chapters/subchapters, correctly referenced figures, and citations of sources for fundamental concepts.

---

## Results & Conclusion

The first draft underwent **20 iterations** based on my feedback.

The final result is in my opinion a solid educational text that successfully combines technical precision with intellectual accessibility. This experiment demonstrated that today's top-tier AI models are **excellent tools for the efficient creation of complex and academically demanding materials**, while also proving that the **human element remains irreplaceable** in this process.
